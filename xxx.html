Who was XXXTENTACION?

Jahseh Dwayne Ricardo Onfroy, also known as XXXTentacion (often abbreviated as XXXTENTACION), was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter who was born on January 23, 1998, and died on June 18, 2018. With his music about despair and loneliness, XXXTentacion developed a cult following among his youthful followers despite being a controversial character due to his well reported legal issues. He is frequently praised by critics and followers for his musical variety, with songs that touch on genres including emo, drill, trap, lo-fi, indie rock, nu metal, hip hop, R&B, and punk rock. He is the all-time bestselling emo rapper and is regarded as a key role in the emo rap and SoundCloud rap scenes that gained popularity in the middle to late 2010s. XXXTentacion was born in Plantation, Florida, but grew up primarily in Lauderhill. After being released from a juvenile detention facility, he started writing music. He soon launched his music career on SoundCloud in 2013, using unconventional rap music styles and techniques like distortion and heavily guitar-backed instrumentals, and drawing inspiration from third-wave emo and grunge. He founded the underground group Members Only in 2014, and he quickly rose to prominence in the SoundCloud rap scene, a type of trap music that incorporates harsh 808s and lo-fi sound. XXXTentacion became well-known because to the song "Look at Me." His self-titled debut album from 2017 has a double platinum certification in the US and debuted at number two on the Billboard 200. His second album? (2018), which is quadruple-platinum in the US, debuted at the top of the Billboard 200. The album's lead song, "Sad!" received Diamond certification from the RIAA in August 202, posthumously peaked at number one on the Billboard Hot 100, and by November 2021 had gathered more than 1.3 billion views on YouTube and 1.7 billion listens on Spotify.
